Timeline of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • January 17, 1942 - Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. was born is Louisville, Kentucky.
  • 1954 - Famously, Clay's bike is stolen at 12 years old. To retaliate, Clay promises to seek revenge and reports the crime to the police station. A policeman directs the future great to Fred Stoner, a boxing trainer.
  • 1960 - Cassius Clay becomes an Olympic gold medalist at Rome Summer Olympics. After returning to his home city of Louisville, KY Clay attempts to eat at a "whites-only" restauraunt. He is refused service and invokes a fight with a white gang. In response to the injustice, he throws his gold medal into the Ohio River.
  • 1964 - Clay becomes the world heavy-weight boxing champion after defeating Sonny Liston against the odds. After the fight, Cassius Clay announces he has became a Black Muslim and also changed his name to Muhammad Ali.
  • April 28, 1967 - Ali famously says "I ain't got no quarrel with those vietcong." while refusing to be inducted into the armed forces during the United States' war in Vietnam.
  • June 20, 1967 - Muhammad is convicted of draft evasion and is consequently:
    • Sentenced to five years in prison
    • Fined $10,000
    • Banned from boxing for three years.
  • March, 1971 - Ali loses to heavyweight champ Joe Frazier by unanimous decision after being knocked down in the 15th round.
  • June, 1971 - Supreme court rules in his favor, reversing the draft-evasion conviction from 1967.
  • 1974 - Ali beats Joe Frazier in January and regains the heavyweight title after knocking out George Foreman in the eighth round of the highly ancipated "Rumble in the Jungle".
  • 1975 - Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier face off the a third time at the "Thrilla in Manila". Ali is deemed victorious when Frazier is unable to come out for the 15th round.
  • 1978 - The Champ loses his belt to 1976 Olympic Champ, Leon Sprinks, before later regaining the title seven months later.
  • June 27, 1979 - Ali announces his retirement.
  • 1984 - Ali is diagnosed with Parkinson's disease.
  • 1996 - Muhammad Ali carries the Olympic torch to begin the Summer Olympics in Atlanta
  • September 13, 1999 - Muhammad is named "Kentucky Athlete of the Century" by the Kentucky Athletic Hall of Fame.
  • 2005 - Ali receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the "Otto Hahn peace medal in Gold" from the United Nations Association of Germany for his work with the United Nations and the american civil rights movement.
  • November 19, 2005 - The Muhammad Ali Center opens in downtown Louisville, Kentucky.
  • June 3, 2016 - Sepsis takes the life of Muhammad Ali at age 74.
